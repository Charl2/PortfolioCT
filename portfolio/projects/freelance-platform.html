Part 1

UX Research

Core Challenge

Optimising the Onboarding Questionnaire

The largest UX research challenge at WEEM was the platform's onboarding questionnaire — the gateway through which consultants and freelancers create their profiles. The brief was clear but demanding: make it as short, coherent, and efficient as possible so users could onboard quickly, while still capturing the maximum amount of useful information.

This required a lot of UX writing — every question had to earn its place. The challenge was constantly balancing brevity against completeness, and clarity against precision.

Approach

I ran a high volume of online surveys and questionnaires to test different question formats, orderings, and phrasings. In parallel, I organised screen-sharing calls directly with consultants and freelancers — they would walk through the live questionnaire on their screen, highlight which questions felt unclear or redundant, and suggest improvements in real time.

These sessions were invaluable: seeing where users paused, re-read, or skipped gave direct signal on where friction lived. Each round fed into a revised version of the questionnaire.

User Research Sessions

Screen-Sharing Interviews

Beyond the questionnaire, I conducted direct research sessions with consultants to surface broader platform pain points. Each participant shared their screen and walked through the live website, pointing out what they would improve — whether in terms of UX flows, information architecture, or missing features.

This qualitative layer complemented the survey data and gave a grounded, contextual view of how real users navigated the product day-to-day.

Personas & User Stories

Segmenting by Tech Affinity

Research findings were synthesised into personas, with a key axis being tech affinity rather than age alone. WEEM's consultant base included experienced professionals less comfortable with digital tools, so personas were built to capture that spectrum — from digital-native freelancers to senior consultants who needed a more guided, low-friction experience.

User stories were then defined for each persona type to prioritise which design decisions would have the broadest positive impact.

Part 2

UX Design

Design Process

Weekly Reviews with CEO & COO

The design work was structured around weekly calls with the CEO and COO. Each session was used to present new screens, validate design directions, and align on visual and functional decisions before moving forward.

This tight feedback loop meant iterations stayed grounded in business intent. Designs were built and revised directly in Figma, with the leadership team guiding both the brand alignment and the feature prioritisation.

Scope

The redesign covered the full platform experience: home page, consultant profiles, FAQ, and settings — all restructured to align with WEEM's brand identity and reduce friction for daily use. Interactive Figma prototypes were produced for each screen, enabling meaningful stakeholder review and usability testing before development handoff.

Conclusion

This project combined two distinct disciplines: deep qualitative and quantitative UX research to sharpen the onboarding experience, and iterative product design in close collaboration with leadership. The research phase surfaced real friction points that the design phase could directly address — making the final product both more usable and more aligned with what WEEM's consultants actually needed.
