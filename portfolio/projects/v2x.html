Project Overview

V2X — Making Bordeaux's Streets Safer

Collision Prevention (V2X — Vehicle to Everything) is a real-life mobility pilot in Bordeaux Métropole aiming to make everyday trips safer for cyclists by enabling real-time alerts between road users (bus drivers, cyclists, cars) through a smartphone-based solution.

The project improved an existing Android APK and then integrated the V2X SDK into the TBM mobility app, so beta testers could receive contextual safety alerts (direction + distance + severity), including a high-severity collision alert powered by Eye-Net algorithms. The solution is now live in Bordeaux.

What I Delivered

Research, Design & Coordination

🔗
Cross-Partner Coordination I supported the Program Manager across the full pilot cycle, acting as the link between Orange, Atos, Eviden, Eye-Net, LabTBMouv', Ville de Bordeaux, and TBM/Keolis — ensuring research, design, and SDK integration moved forward in sync.

UX Research — Real-World Simulation (2 days / 22 users) I designed and ran a groundbreaking real-world simulation over two days with 22 participants across different mobility profiles. Built the protocol (survey + scenario testing), recruited by persona, and ensured test conditions reflected daily commuting realities. The goal: not just "does it work?" but "is it understandable, acceptable, and useful enough to be used every day?"
📊
Data Analysis → Decision-Making Deliverables I turned a large volume of qualitative and quantitative feedback into structured, decision-ready analysis — categorising issues by feature area, type, and frequency, then building a stakeholder presentation translating this into prioritised recommendations for app modifications and alert strategy.
🎨
Product Design & Tester Experience I designed the TBM app integration in Figma: alert integration, onboarding content, Settings/Parameters area, RGPD and consent patterns, and a tiered alert personalization approach to prevent "alert fatigue" — keeping alerts meaningful, trusted, and actually used.
Product Design

Figma Prototypes & App Integration

Alert Types

Three levels of contextual safety alerts — each designed with a distinct severity, icon, and copy to communicate direction, distance, and urgency clearly at a glance, without overwhelming daily users.

Onboarding Flow

A step-by-step onboarding sequence guiding testers through permissions, RGPD consent, and alert personalization settings — designed to build trust and reduce drop-off before the first real alert is ever received.
