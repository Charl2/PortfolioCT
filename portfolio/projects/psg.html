My Role

Product Manager, Immersiv.io

I shipped two major features on a hard deadline: a live 180° stadium view (watch the match from inside Parc des Princes) and a 3D tabletop mode. Both launched for PSG vs. Marseille on January 5th, and again for PSG vs. Monaco — both live broadcasts ran without a single critical failure. PSG promoted the app heavily on LinkedIn. It was both a PR success and a clean technical execution.

I ran weekly meetings with PSG's product manager and media director, and biweekly sessions with the Google Android XR lead. I brought structure to every meeting — organized slides with clear ownership tables (who does what, when, how, and why). When blockers surfaced, I translated technical issues into language C-level could act on, then followed up by email until resolved.

Project Overview

Redefining How Matches Are Followed

The PSG XR app was funded by Google to support the launch of the Android XR headset in the US and South Korea, developed in collaboration with Samsung on the hardware side. From the home page, fans access live matches and replays in both 2D and 180°, match highlights, and exclusive premium content via a MyParis Premium account — enhanced with real-time stats and a 3D tabletop view.

The key differentiator is the 180° live and replay viewing mode. With five cameras installed inside Parc des Princes, users watch matches from within the stadium — with automatic camera switching and spatial audio designed to capture the energy of a live PSG match.

Product Decisions

How I Shaped the Product

01 Putting 180° Front and Centre on the Homepage
Challenge

The homepage needed to surface the right content clearly — but the app's core value proposition, the 180° stadium view, risked getting buried among highlights, stats, and other content.

Decision

I pushed for a dedicated right column exclusively for 180° content. Working closely with the designer, I advocated for a layout where the app's differentiator was immediately visible — not something users had to discover.

Outcome

The 180° feature was front and centre from day one. Fans arriving at the home page immediately saw what made the app unique, which supported both adoption and PSG's marketing positioning.

Core differentiator made immediately visible
02 Redesigning the Match List Around a Licensing Problem
Challenge

When we implemented SSO to protect premium full-replay rights, it had an unintended side effect: free users lost access to highlights and stats — content that should have been freely available to everyone.

Decision

I redesigned the entire match list user flow to cleanly separate free and premium content. Free users retained access to highlights and stats; premium users unlocked full replays. This required both UX work and stakeholder alignment with PSG.

Outcome

A licensing constraint that could have degraded the free experience was turned into a clear, functional tiering model — protecting both user trust and content rights.

Free experience preserved without compromising rights
03 Cutting the Video Gallery Filters Down to What Matters
Challenge

The video gallery had accumulated too many filter options. The result was cognitive overload — users faced with too many choices before they could even start browsing content.

Decision

I consolidated the filters down to the essentials: match highlights and immersive content. Everything else was noise. Fewer choices, faster access to what users actually came for.

Outcome

A simpler, faster browsing experience — and a cleaner visual presentation of PSG's content library, which supported the premium positioning of the app overall.

Cognitive load reduced, browsing speed improved
04 Making the Most of XR's Visual Space
Challenge

The tabletop and live views were underusing what makes XR different. Stats and lineup panels defaulted to closed — treating the headset like a phone screen, where screen real estate is scarce.

Decision

I pushed for visual stats and a momentum tracker on the timeline slider, and advocated for both lineup and stats panels to open by default. On XR, you have comfortable visual space to absorb more — keeping panels closed just adds a click with no benefit.

Outcome

Users land directly in an information-rich view that takes advantage of the medium. One less interaction barrier — and a live experience that feels genuinely spatial rather than a phone app on a big screen.

Richer default view, one fewer click

Conclusion

The PSG project was about execution under real stakes — live matches, real fans, a Google partnership, and no room for bugs. Shipping 180° live stadium view and tabletop mode for PSG vs. Marseille and PSG vs. Monaco without a critical failure on either broadcast wasn't just a technical milestone. It was proof that the right PM focus — unblocking, aligning, and pushing for the right decisions — is what separates a good product from one that works when it matters.

What I'm most proud of is the problems that didn't make it to launch day. The licensing issue that could have broken the free experience. The filters that would have slowed everyone down. The blockers that stayed stuck until I translated them clearly enough for C-level to act. Those are the decisions that don't show up in a demo — but they're what made the demo work.
