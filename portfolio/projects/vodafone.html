I joined the Vodafone 5G AR Stadium App project as Product Manager on Immersiv.io's side. My main responsibilities were monitoring app function with the backend team, maintaining client contact to ensure satisfaction, and making sure the app was updated ahead of each Ireland rugby game — with all team avatars and season stats ready for each fixture.

Project Overview

The app was developed to enhance the in-stadium fan experience at Aviva Stadium during Six Nations matches in Dublin. Leveraging Vodafone's 5G connectivity, it delivers real-time AR overlays of live match data, player insights, and key events directly onto the pitch through fans' mobile devices.

Designed for use before, during, and after the match, the experience combines AR visuals, live statistics, and interactive content — while accounting for real-world constraints like seating position and pitch visibility. The app also provides a non-AR fallback, ensuring accessibility for all spectators.

Experience Overview

Before, During & After the Match

🏟️
Stadium Onboarding & Pitch Detection Fans select their seating area to assess pitch visibility, then scan the field to enable AR anchoring. If detection fails, users are seamlessly redirected to a non-AR experience with full access to stats, lineups, and match data.
📡
Live Match AR Experience Real-time AR visuals overlay team lineups, player positioning, and dynamic animations tied to key events — tries, conversions, penalties, scrums and lineouts — with contextual data like kick distance and success probability.
👤
Player & Team Insights Player profiles with live and season stats, biographical data (height, weight, caps, club), and 3D AR overlays of lineups and full squads on the pitch — accessible at any point and adapting dynamically during live events.
🔄
Match Analysis & Replay During breaks, halftime, and post-match: timelines of key events, AR replay animations, team stat comparisons, and heatmaps. AR visualizations directly on the pitch transform the stadium into an interactive analysis space.

Conclusion

The Vodafone 5G Stadium App demonstrated how augmented reality and real-time data can meaningfully enhance the in-stadium rugby experience without distracting from the live game. Delivering a live, AR-driven product in a stadium environment required careful consideration of user context, connectivity, and usability under real-world constraints — including a robust non-AR fallback to ensure accessibility.

The result was a scalable model for how AR and 5G can transform live sports attendance — turning the stadium into an interactive layer of the broadcast itself.
