XR Experience with ESPN & Meta

Courtside from Anywhere in the World

This collaboration between ESPN and Meta brought the US Open into virtual reality — letting fans explore a 3D tabletop court, watch matches from any angle, pause and replay key moments, and access real-time performance stats that previously only existed as broadcast overlays. The project showcased how immersive technology can redefine fan engagement and set a new standard for live sports.

Mobile & Web Experience

With US Open & IBM

Fans could also access the XR experience via the US Open mobile app and website. While it did not stream live video, it offered the same 3D player animations, interactive stats, and replay features as the VR platform.

Users could revisit points, switch angles, and explore performance data — making the experience widely accessible, while the Meta Quest version delivered the most immersive courtside perspective.

Experience Overview

Four Ways to Experience the Match

🎾
Live & Interactive Tactical View A fully interactive 3D tabletop recreation of each match with lifelike player avatars. A timeline tool lets fans pause, rewind, and replay key moments — combined with real-time visual insights for a next-generation viewing experience.
📐
Personalized Viewing Experience Complete control over perspective: multi-angle zoom, tabletop or full-screen mode, free movement around the court, or instant jumps to preset views. Every fan crafts their own way of watching.
📊
Immersive XR Stats Panels All stats built into the immersive environment — aces, serves, winners, double faults, updated in real time. With a simple gesture, fans open dedicated panels to explore the game without leaving the 3D view.
🗺️
3D Interactive Stats on the Court Animated first and second serve placements, shot tracking by width and depth, serve return contact points, and player movement heatmaps — displayed directly on the 3D court across tabletop, mobile, and desktop.
