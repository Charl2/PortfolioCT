Part 01 — XR Coaching App
My Role

Product Manager & Contract Owner

The NHL was Immersiv.io's largest client, and my mandate was to renew and grow the relationship. I owned the stakeholder relationship directly — weekly C-level meetings with the NHL's Director of Innovation, Director of Data, and coaching leads, plus daily Slack contact to flag data issues, route them to the right people, and present UX decisions with clear options for evaluation.

Internally, I defined scope, structured the product backlog, and owned the roadmap. Every decision discussed informally on Slack was formalized at the next weekly — keeping alignment tight and documentation consistent. That cadence was key to delivering the $1.4M contract renewal across two NHL seasons.

Project Overview

Developed in partnership with Apple for Apple Vision Pro, the NHL XR Coaching App gives coaching staff a spatial tool to review games in 3D, analyze player performance, and present tactical insights. Within the first season, 12 teams adopted the app — with the Edmonton Oilers, the best-performing team that season, among the most engaged users.

The use cases that emerged told the story. Teams used the app to scout players, settle coaching disagreements, and analyze referee decisions. In one case, a coaching staff used the Player POV feature to review a goalkeeper's field of vision on a disputed play — and identified a foul they should have challenged live. That wasn't a hypothetical benefit. It was the product working exactly as intended.

Part 02 — Fan Experience
My Role

Fixing What Blocked the Features — Before Fans Arrived

My goal was clear: get the app ready for fan testing without wasting it on surface-level issues. I led the kickoff, conducted a full product audit, and applied one core principle — fix what's blocking existing features, not chasing new ones. That framing shaped every prioritization decision.

I structured a ranked improvement plan and presented it to C-level stakeholders. The result: two fan testing sessions in the US and Canada — each 1h30 long — run by the NHL's dedicated fan research team alongside third-party specialist consultants. The product had to be good enough that testers could evaluate the concept, not fight the interface.

Project Overview

3D Hockey for Every Fan

Building on the XR capabilities of the Coaching App, this fan-facing experience is accessible through the official NHL mobile app. Fans follow games live or in replay through a 3D recreation of the ice — in a realistic or gamified style — exploring player-level stats in real time and engaging with matches in a more immersive, data-driven way.

Product Decisions

How I Shaped the Product

01 Solving Motion Sickness Without Losing Coaching Value
Challenge

Pure first-person POV caused motion sickness on the headset — camera tracking felt too fast. But coaches needed to see exactly what a player sees to analyse field of vision and on-ice decision-making.

Decision

I pushed for a hybrid: third-person by default so coaches watch the play develop without nausea. When they pause, it switches to first-person — showing the exact player perspective at that moment.

Outcome

Coaches got both — full play context and precise POV analysis. Teams used it for scouting, settling coaching disputes, and in one case identified a foul they should have challenged live.

Real-world use by coaching staff in first season
02 Keeping the Timeline on One Screen
Challenge

The original timeline was so cluttered it effectively blocked the feature. The proposed fix was a separate screen — cleaner, but it would break context and add a navigation layer.

Decision

Keep it on one screen, reorganized by NHL periods. Replaced the multi-step period selector with left/right arrows (one tap instead of two). Removed forced reloads when switching periods so users can browse freely.

Outcome

Users could navigate the full match without losing context or disrupting playback. The feature went from effectively blocked to the core interaction of the mobile experience.

Core UX unblocked for fan testing
03 From 50% to 100% Player Name Visibility
Challenge

When a player received the puck, their highlighted name was hidden by rink boundary rendering roughly half the time — undermining trust in the data layer for coaching use.

Decision

I pushed to prioritise this fix as part of the pre-testing audit. Fast to fix, high impact — player identification is foundational to every coaching and scouting workflow in the app.

Outcome

100% visibility after the fix. Teams used the corrected view for scouting, coaching disputes, and in one case to retroactively identify a foul that should have been challenged live.

100% visibility — up from ~50%
04 Fix Blockers First — Not New Features
Challenge

The mobile app had existing features that didn't work properly — and there was pressure to keep adding new capabilities before the scheduled fan testing sessions.

Decision

I led the kickoff with one guiding principle: fix what blocks existing value before chasing new features. I ran a full product audit, ranked issues by impact and feasibility, and structured the first development cycle around that list.

Outcome

Two fan testing sessions — US and Canada, 1h30 each — run by NHL's specialist fan research team alongside third-party consultants. Testers evaluated the concept, not the interface.

2 formal fan testing sessions delivered
Experience Overview

Core Features Across Both Platforms

🏒
Game & Team Navigation Select teams, games, and specific periods to review across mobile and Vision Pro. Past games persist across sessions, enabling efficient repeat usage for both fans and coaching staff.
⏱️
Timeline-Driven Match Exploration A fully interactive timeline with play, pause, rewind, fast-forward, and frame-by-frame navigation. Filter by events (goals, shots, faceoffs, shifts), add bookmarks, and navigate matches for both casual review and tactical analysis.
📊
Player Performance Insights Player-level insights surface directly within the 3D recreation — shifts, key events, time on ice, and contextual stats. Supports fan engagement on mobile and professional scouting and coaching workflows on Vision Pro.
🎯
Viewing Modes & Immersive Interaction Coaches can inhabit a player's perspective or toggle between strategic cameras with granular control over FOV (Field of View), hip orientation, and proximity — providing a precise behavioral dataset for elite player-coach film sessions and scouting.

Conclusion

The NHL project taught me what it means to hold both ends of the product simultaneously — a fan experience heading into real user testing, and a professional tool in daily use by coaching staff at the highest level of the sport. The constraint in both cases was the same: time is limited, so fix what blocks value first.

What I'm proud of isn't the feature list. It's the decisions that made the features usable — the hybrid POV that solved motion sickness, the timeline that stayed on one screen, the player name visible every time. And the relationship work that held it all together: a $1.4M renewal built on weekly alignment, daily responsiveness, and a product that coaches actually used.
